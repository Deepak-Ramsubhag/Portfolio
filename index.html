Projects

Kappa Algorithm

The Kappa Algorithm is a novel neighbor-based machine learning approach. I have enhanced its performance by optimizing distance metrics and loss functions to align with the algorithm’s complexity and the statistical properties of data. My innovative parameter tuning techniques have improved its efficiency, resulting in three first-author IEEE conference papers on regression tasks and identifying key areas for further development. I continue to expand its application to classification, anomaly detection, advanced optimization methods, and deeper theoretical analyses.

Segmentation and Classification of Planktonic Images

This project leverages deep learning to automate the segmentation and classification of plankton in microscopic underwater images from the Caribbean. As a data scientist on this team, I focus on designing and refining advanced image segmentation techniques to enhance both accuracy and reliability.

Morphological Analysis and Classification of Mosquitoes

As a machine learning engineer, I am dedicated to the morphological analysis and classification of mosquito species native to Trinidad and Tobago. By employing deep learning frameworks, I work to automate species identification and population assessments, thereby reducing reliance on time-consuming and costly genetic testing methods.

AI for Foliar Disease Detection

I am developing AI solutions to detect, classify, and predict the severity of foliar diseases in Caribbean plants. My contributions include optimizing the project framework, building a web application to support real-time disease monitoring and decision-making, developing new and efficient feature extraction methods, and applying traditional machine learning models to achieve highly accurate classification results, with journal articles currently under review.

AI4COASTS (AI in Coastal Resilience)

As the lead data scientist and machine learning engineer on a funded coastal resilience initiative, I drive efforts to harness AI for identifying regions at risk, formulating actionable improvement strategies, and synthesizing insights from diverse data sources.

AI in Climate Change: Digital Twins, LLMs

In this large-scale project, I employ AI technologies to address climate change challenges. My work involves integrating Digital Twins and Large Language Models (LLMs) to enhance flood resilience, support mitigation efforts, and inform policymaking in Small Island Developing States (SIDS).
