Projects

Kappa Algorithm

The Kappa Algorithm is a novel neighbor-based machine learning approach. My research has identified critical areas for improvement, and I have significantly enhanced its performance by optimizing distance metrics and loss functions in alignment with the statistical properties of data. I have also improved its computational efficiency through innovative parameter tuning techniques. This work has resulted in three first-author IEEE conference publications on regression tasks and has uncovered key directions for further advancement, which are currently under exploration. My ongoing research extends its applications to classification, anomaly detection, and theoretical analysis of the algorithm.

Segmentation and Classification of Planktonic Images

This project employs deep learning techniques to automate the segmentation and classification of plankton in microscopic underwater images from the Caribbean. As a data scientist on the team, I focus on designing and refining segmentation methods tailored to environments with limited labeled data, as commonly encountered in Caribbean research settings.

Morphological Analysis and Classification of Mosquitoes

As a machine learning engineer, I am dedicated to the morphological analysis and classification of mosquito species native to Trinidad and Tobago. By employing deep learning frameworks, I work to automate species identification and population assessments, thereby reducing reliance on time-consuming and costly genetic testing methods.

AI for Foliar Disease Detection

As a data scientist on a large-scale initiative, I apply AI tools to address climate change challenges in Small Island Developing States (SIDS). This work focuses on the integration of Digital Twins and Large Language Models (LLMs) for flood resilience, disaster mitigation, and evidencebased policymaking. A book chapter based on this project has been accepted for publication.

AI in Climate Change: Digital Twins, LLMs

In this large-scale project, I employ AI technologies to address climate change challenges. My work involves integrating Digital Twins and Large Language Models (LLMs) to enhance flood resilience, support mitigation efforts, and inform policymaking in Small Island Developing States (SIDS).

AI4COASTS (AI in Coastal Resilience)

As the lead data scientist and machine learning engineer on this funded coastal resilience initiative, I lead efforts to leverage AI for identifying high-risk regions, synthesizing multi-source data, and formulating actionable strategies to enhance coastal adaptation and sustainability.
